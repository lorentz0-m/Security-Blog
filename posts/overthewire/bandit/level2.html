OverTheWire Bandit Level 2 Write‑up

Updated: 14/04/2024
| 0.5 min read

The file stored in our home directory this time happens to have spaces in its filename, which would normally cause a problem, but you can easily circumvent it by wrapping the entire filename in quotes/double-quotes, or escaping each space character with a backslash.

tip: most shells have auto-completion enabled, meaning that you only have to type part of a filename, and by hitting tab the shell will complete the name for you, saving you lots of valuable time
Method 1: 
				
					bandit2@bandit:~$ cat "spaces in this filename"
					aBZ0W5EmUfAf7kHTQeOwd8bauFJ2lAiG
Method 2: 
				
					bandit2@bandit:~$ cat spaces\ in\ this\ filename
					aBZ0W5EmUfAf7kHTQeOwd8bauFJ2lAiG
Onto level 3!
